Buttermilk Chicken Tenders

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • 1.5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tbsp seasoned salt
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tbs parsley flakes, dried
  • 2 cups vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Place chicken breasts between plastic wrap and pound them to about ½" thick. Cut into 1" strips.
  2. Marinate the chicken strips in buttermilk for 1 hour in the refrigerator.
  3. Mix flour, seasoned salt, paprika, and parsley flakes in one bowl. Beat two eggs in another bowl. Heat oil in a pan to 350°F (medium heat for about 5 minutes).
  4. Remove chicken from buttermilk. Dredge each strip in the flour mix, shake off excess, dip in the egg, let excess drip off, and dredge in flour again. Set aside on a plate.
  5. Carefully place coated chicken in hot oil. Fry for 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through (internal temperature of 165°F).
  6. Remove chicken from oil and drain on paper towels.
  7. Enjoy!

Sweet and Spicy Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 lbs of fully cooked crispy chicken strips
  • ½ cup of light brown sugar
  • 4 tbsp of honey BBQ sauce
  • 3 tsp of hot sauce
  • ¼ cup of water

Instructions:

  1. Bake the chicken following the instructions on the packaging.
  2. In a saucepan, combine water, brown sugar, BBQ sauce, and hot sauce. Heat over low to medium heat, stirring until the sauce is well mixed.
  3. Once the chicken is baked, pour the sauce over it, making sure it's evenly coated.
  4. Enjoy!

Southern Fried Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut up (or 8 pieces of your favorite cuts of chicken)
  • 4 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 tbsp hot sauce
  • 2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • peanut oil (for frying)
  • flaked salt (for serving, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine cut-up chicken, salt, garlic powder, onion powder, buttermilk, and hot sauce in a bowl. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight is best).
  2. In a bowl, combine flour, cornstarch, salt, paprika, and pepper.
  3. Fill a deep fryer with peanut oil (or another neutral oil) and heat to 350°F.
  4. Let excess buttermilk drip off each chicken piece, then coat it thoroughly in the flour mixture. Shake off any excess. Let the coating become slightly pasty before frying.
  5. Carefully add 3-4 pieces of chicken to the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the fryer. Fry until golden brown, turning every few minutes.
  6. Use a meat thermometer to ensure the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F. Dark meat may take 12-14 minutes, while white meat takes 8-10 minutes.
  7. Remove the fried chicken and place it on a paper towel-lined tray. Sprinkle with flaked salt. Repeat steps 4-7 for the remaining chicken pieces.
  8. Enjoy!

Honey Buffalo Wings

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1½ tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1½ tsp onion powder or dried minced onion
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • ¼ cup hot sauce
  • ½ cup honey
  • 1 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 tbsp molasses (optional but recommended)
  • 4 lbs fresh chicken wings, patted dry

Instructions:

  1. Combine the rub ingredients in a small bowl and stir well.
  2. Spray your slow cooker with non-stick spray. Add the chicken wings and sprinkle the rub over them. Use your hands to massage the rub into the wings.
  3. In the empty rub bowl, whisk together the sauce ingredients. Pour the sauce over the wings and toss gently to coat.
  4. Cover the slow cooker and cook on HIGH for 2 hours, or on LOW for 3-4 hours.
  5. Enjoy!

Buffalo Chicken Tenders

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken breasts, boneless, skinless
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tsp hot sauce
  • 2 large eggs beaten
  • 2 cups flour
  • 2 ½ tsp salt
  • ¾ tsp pepper
  • ⅛ tsp paprika
  • ⅛ tsp garlic powder
  • ⅛ tsp baking powder
  • canola oil for frying
  • ⅔ cup Franks Red Hot pepper sauce
  • 1 stick unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • ¼ tsp Worcestershire sauce

Instructions:

  1. Cut the chicken breasts into four tenders each. Soak them in buttermilk and hot sauce for 30 minutes.
  2. In one bowl, whisk eggs. In another bowl, combine flour and spices.
  3. Dip each chicken tender in the buttermilk, then in the flour mixture, then in the eggs, and finally back into the flour mixture. Shake off excess flour and place on a baking sheet. Repeat for all tenders.
  4. Heat oil to 350°F in a Dutch oven (oil should be 3 inches deep).
  5. Fry the chicken tenders in small batches for 5-7 minutes, or until golden brown.
  6. Combine the buffalo wing sauce ingredients in a saucepan and bring to a boil while whisking.
  7. Dip the fried chicken tenders in the buffalo wing sauce and serve immediately.
  8. Enjoy!

Fried Scallops

  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • 1 tsp salt

  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp pepper
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1½ cup breadcrumbs

Instructions:

  1. Rinse scallops under cold water and pat dry. Remove any tough white muscle if present.
  2. In one bowl, mix together flour, cornstarch, salt, garlic powder, paprika, and pepper.
  3. In another bowl, whisk eggs and water.
  4. In a third bowl, add breadcrumbs.
  5. Dip each scallop in the flour mixture, then the egg mixture, and finally the breadcrumbs.
  6. Heat oil in a pan to 250-300°F. Fry the scallops in batches (6-10 at a time) until golden brown, about 1-2 minutes.
  7. Remove scallops from the oil and place on a paper-towel lined plate. Repeat with remaining scallops. Serve immediately with cocktail sauce, tartar sauce, and fresh lemons.
  8. Enjoy!

Fried Calamari

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb cleaned calamari tubes cut into rings, and tentacles left whole
  • juice of ½ a lemon
  • 2 cups flour
  • ½ tsp cayenne
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ cup buttermilk
  • tallow for deep frying
  • marinara sauce, lemon wedges, or aioli, for serving, optional

Instructions:

  1. Soak the calamari in lemon juice for 15 minutes.
  2. Mix flour, cayenne, paprika, and salt in a bowl. Divide this mixture into two bowls.
  3. Place buttermilk or milk in a separate bowl.
  4. Heat tallow or your preferred oil to 375°F in a deep fryer or large cast iron pot.
  5. Remove the calamari from the lemon juice. Dredge each piece in flour, then buttermilk, and then flour again.
  6. Fry the calamari in batches for 2-3 minutes, until golden brown. Drain on paper towels and immediately salt generously.
  7. Enjoy with marinara sauce and lemon wedges.

Crispy Shrimp Tempura

Ingredients:

  • ½ lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for deep frying
  • 2 ¼ cup all purpose flour
  • ⅓ cup ice water
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • 1 large egg
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp white sugar
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 5 ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a deep fryer to 375°F.
  2. Mix flour, ice water, cornstarch, egg, salt, sugar, and baking powder in a bowl. Add ice cubes to the batter to keep it fresh and prevent it from becoming sticky.
  3. Place flour in a separate bowl. Coat each shrimp with flour. Dip the floured shrimp into the batter.
  4. Carefully place a few shrimp in the hot oil. Fry for 1 minute or until golden brown on all sides.
  5. Remove the tempura shrimp from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  6. Enjoy!

Crispy Garlic Parmesan Salmon

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs side wild salmon fillet
  • ⅔ cup plain breadcrumbs
  • ⅔ cup parmesan cheese grated
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic minced
  • ⅓ cup butter melted
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 pinch pepper
  • 2 lemon wedges to serve

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with foil.
  2. Combine breadcrumbs, parmesan cheese, parsley and garlic in a bowl.
  3. Add melted butter and season with salt and pepper. Mix well until the breadcrumbs absorb the butter.
  4. Place the salmon fillet, skin side down, on the baking sheet. Spread the topping evenly over the salmon, pressing it in to coat completely. Lightly spray the salmon with cooking oil spray.
  5. Bake uncovered for 12-15 minutes (depending on the thickness of the salmon), until the topping is golden brown and the salmon flakes easily with a fork.
  6. Serve the salmon with lemon wedges and enjoy with a salad, steamed vegetables, rice, or mashed potatoes.

Bang Bang Shrimp

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup vegetable oil, or more, as needed
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • ¾ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp dried basil
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • 1 pound medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • ¼ cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tbsp sweet chili sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp hot sauce
  • kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine mayonnaise, sweet chili sauce, honey, and rice vinegar in a bowl. Set aside.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together buttermilk, flour, cornstarch, egg, hot sauce, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine breadcrumbs, onion powder, garlic powder, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  4. Dip each shrimp into the buttermilk mixture, then dredge in the breadcrumb mixture, pressing to coat.
  5. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Fry the shrimp in batches until golden brown and crispy (about 2-3 minutes). Drain on paper towels.
  6. Drizzle the shrimp with the sweet chili sauce and serve immediately.
  7. Enjoy!

Fried Onion Rings

Ingredients:

  • 3 onions, sliced into rings
  • 1 quart buttermilk
  • 3 cups flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp pepper
  • 2 tsp mexican oregano
  • Oil for frying, at least 1 inch deep in pan

Instructions:

  1. Place the onion rings in a bowl of buttermilk and let them soak for at least an hour.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking powder, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Remove the onion rings from the buttermilk, one at a time, and coat them thoroughly in the flour mixture.
  4. Heat oil to 375°F. Fry the coated onion rings in batches until golden brown and crispy (about 2-3 minutes).
  5. Remove the onion rings from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain.
  6. Enjoy!

Egg Salad

Ingredients:

  • 6 whole eggs in shell, brought to room temperature
  • 4 quarts of water
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 4-6 tbsp good quality mayonnaise
  • Pinch of table salt
  • Pinch of white pepper

Instructions:

  • Fill a pot with water and bring it to a boil. Add a splash of vinegar. Gently lower the eggs into the boiling water, one at a time.
  • Bring the water back to a boil and cook for 5 minutes. Turn off the heat and let the eggs sit in the hot water for 13 minutes.
  • Drain the water and shake the pot gently to crack the shells. Peel the eggs under cool running water.
  • Chop the peeled eggs into a bowl. Add mayonnaise, salt, and pepper to taste. Stir and adjust seasonings as needed.
  • Cover the bowl and refrigerate until the egg salad is chilled. Serve on lettuce with your favorite bread.
  • Enjoy!

Parmesan Cauliflower Bites

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cauliflower florets washed and thoroughly dried; about 1 small head of cauliflower
  • 2 tbsp butter melted
  • ½ cup parmesan cheese finely grated
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Set your oven to 425°F.
  2. Toss cauliflower florets with melted butter in a bowl. Make sure all the florets are coated.
  3. Combine parmesan cheese, paprika, and pepper. Sprinkle the mixture over the buttered cauliflower.
  4. Arrange the cauliflower in a single layer on a baking sheet. Bake for 20 minutes, or until the edges start to crisp.
  5. Serve the cauliflower hot from the oven.
  6. Enjoy!

Onion Petals

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 2 tbsp prepared horseradish
  • 1 clove garlic minced
  • ½ tsp kosher salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp smoked paprika
  • pinch cayenne pepper
  • 1 large sweet onion
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsps seasoned salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp cayenne
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Combine mayonnaise, sour cream, ketchup, horseradish, garlic, salt, pepper, paprika, and cayenne in a bowl. Whisk until smooth and refrigerate until serving.
  2. Cut the onion in half, then into quarters. Cut the quarters in half again. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, combine flour, seasoned salt, black pepper, garlic powder, cayenne, onion powder, and smoked paprika.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together buttermilk and egg.
  5. Place onion petals in a bag with ¼ cup of the dry flour mixture. Shake to coat.Dip coated petals in the buttermilk mixture. Place petals back in the dry flour mixture and coat well.
  6. Heat oil in a deep fryer or skillet to 350°F. Fry the coated onion petals for 3-4 minutes until golden brown.
  7. Drain the fried petals on paper towels and place on a wire rack to prevent sogginess. Serve with the sauce.
  8. Enjoy!

Crispy Baked Potato Wedges

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds Russet potatoes (6 smallish or 4 medium), scrubbed clean
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley, optional

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Cut potatoes into wedges (8 wedges per potato).
  2. Place potato wedges in a bowl and cover with hot water. Let soak for 10 minutes.
  3. Drain potatoes and pat dry. Place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat.
  4. Bake for 30 minutes. Flip the wedges and bake for another 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Sprinkle with parsley (optional) and enjoy hot!
